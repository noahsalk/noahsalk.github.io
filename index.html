About Me

My Motivation

Revolutionizing our energy infrastructure will lead to a clean, sustainable, efficient, and peaceful future. While the political tide turns and begins to accept renewable energy and electrified transportation, technology leaders have the opportunity to reimagine everything related to power generation, usage, and delivery. This could mean developing power dense electric machines to power commercial airliners, using the potential of superconducting technology to efficiently generate power from offshore wind, eliminating lossy, heavy, and expensive magnetic iron from transformers, or even shifting from HVAC transmission to HVDC. We've come a long way since the foundation of our current grid was first conceptualized. It's time to step into the 21st century and use all the tools at our disposal to create a brighter future.

My Background

I received my B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign with a concentration in power electro-mechanics. Advised by Prof. Kiruba Haran, I performed research as an undergraduate on novel fully superconducting electric machines both for NASA's CHEETA commercial aircraft concept and for an NSF-funded 10 MW wind turbine. During my senior year, I began working at a research park based startup, Hinetics. At Hinetics, I manage the build of a prototype 300 kW slotless PM motor with best-in-class power density for a Phase II NASA SBIR. I also lead the efforts to develop a transient power extraction tool for an e-VTOL concept under the Air Force 'Agility Prime' initiative.

I started my Ph.D studies at Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Fall 2020 and began working under Dr. Chathan Cooke to develop high-efficiency air-core resonant power transformers for solar power conversion.
